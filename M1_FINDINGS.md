# M1 — NIFTY Heston instability & the short-end gap (results, full sample)

Full run: **355 sampled dates, 2020-01-01 → 2026-07-08** (weekly+monthly bhavcopy, old+new format).
Heston calibrated to tenors ≥14d; short-end miss measured separately. Reference pricer, vega-weighted
IV RMSE, DE→L-BFGS-B. Auto-generated by `experiments/run_m1_instability.py` → `data/m1/`.

## Calibration quality — regime-dependent
- Median fit IV RMSE **51 bps** over the full sample (was 33 on the calm 2024-26 subset).
- By year (median RMSE): 2020 ~139 · 2021 ~58 · 2022 ~70 · 2023 ~30 · 2024 ~70 · 2025-26 ~33.
- Fit quality **is** the regime signal: Heston fits calm markets to ~30 bps and strains in stress
  (2020-21) with ρ pegging −0.95 (it cannot make skew steeper than ρ→−1).

## Quality tiers & the M2 training set
| tier | rule | ~count | use |
|---|---|---|---|
| clean | RMSE ≤ 100 bps | ~55% | train (full weight) |
| stressed | 100 < RMSE ≤ 200 | ~35% | train (down-weight by 1/RMSE) |
| unfittable (crash) | RMSE > 200 | **9 dates** | **exclude** |

The 9 excluded dates are the contiguous COVID crash **2020-03-11 → 2020-04-29** (v0 pegged 0.4–1.0,
σ_v→2.5, ρ→bounds, RMSE 255–633, short-gap up to ~3000 bps). Heston cannot represent an inverted
crash term structure — recorded, not silently dropped (D7). `rmse_bps` is kept per date as a training
weight / feature.

## The short-end Heston gap (M2's target)
Heston (≥14d) IV RMSE on the <14d smiles it can't reach:
- Level (clean dates): **median ~165 bps calm, ~220–250 bps in 2020–21 stress**, event spikes >600,
  and (crash, excluded) up to ~3000.
- **State-dependent**: quarterly medians ran 43 bps (calm 2025Q4) → 310 (2025Q1) → ~250 (2020-21).
  Correlation with ATM vol ≈ 0.3 — a real but nonlinear dependence, i.e. a market-state-conditioned
  correction is the right tool, not a constant local-vol bump.

## Regimes now covered (why the backfill mattered)
COVID crash & recovery (2020), reflation/steep-skew (2021), rate-hike selloff (2022), calm bull
(2023), and 2024-26. Five distinct vol regimes — the diversity M2 needs.

## Interview line (with numbers)
"Across 2020–2026 NIFTY, single Heston fits ≥2-week tenors to ~30 bps in calm markets but mis-prices
the sub-2-week skew by ~1.6 vol points typically and 3–6 in stress — and it structurally cannot fit
a crash term structure at all. My NN supplies exactly that state-dependent short-end correction inside
a no-arb SDE."
